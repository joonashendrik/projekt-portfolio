Michael Theodore "Mickey" Mouse is a cartoon character created in 1928 by The Walt Disney Company, who also serves as the brand's mascot.
Honors

Mickey Mouse has received ten nominations for the Academy Award for best animated short film;

four of Mickey's cartoons were included in the book The 50 Greatest Cartoons;

On November 18, 1978, in honor of his 50th anniversary, Mickey became the first cartoon character to have a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame;

Mickey Mouse was appointed at Moomba festival as their King of Moomba (1977).

Mickey received the honor as Grand Marshal of the Tournament of Roses Parade on New Year's Day 2005 as first cartoon character.

HISTORY

Mickey was first seen in a test screening of the cartoon short Plane Crazy, on May 15, 1928 In Mickey's early films he was often characterized not as a hero, but as an ineffective young suitor to Minnie Mouse. The Barn Dance (March 14, 1929) is the first time in which Mickey is turned down by Minnie in favor of Pete. In the 1950s, Mickey became more known for his appearances on television, particularly with The Mickey Mouse Club. Many of his theatrical cartoon shorts were rereleased on television series, various forms of the Walt Disney anthology television series, and on home video.
